About

MakeMIT is a hardware hackathon that allows undergraduates to showcase their innovative spirit through large-scale projects, ranging from a driver-exhaustion moniter, to a toaster that prints your morning news. Our fast-paced structure produces an atmosphere of creativity and collaboration as makers work in teams of up to five to create their product. MakeMIT provides cutting edge technology, tools, materials, and mentorship, empowering students to turn their crazy ideas into reality.

FAQ

▾ When is MakeMIT?

This year, we will be extending MakeMIT over 2 weeks (March 6-21) in order to facilitate our new virtual medium. These weeks will be split into both a design and build phase, with participants having time to order their materials somewhere in the middle. Additionally, before the main event, we are dedicating a full week to tech talks, workshops, and demos, which are open to participants and the entire MIT community.

▾ How is MakeMIT going to work these year since it is virtual?

Every Maker will submit project proposals and we will choose finalists who will be able to ask for some materials to fulfill their project. Finally, we will judge the creations and announce a winner!

▾ Who can participate?

All undergraduate students are welcome. While we aren’t accepting graduate students as makers, we would love to have graduate students as event volunteers! Contact us to get involved.

▾ What do I need?

Other than your own creativity and enthusiasm, nothing is absolutely necessary. Building materials will be covered by MakeMIT after the design phase. Just come with some cool ideas and a readiness to learn.

▾ How can I access materials or tools to build?

There are many options. Firstly, Make MIT will subsidize up to $500 worth of purchased tools and materials that are bought during MakeWeek. You are also free to use any personal tools you currently have access to. Unfortunately, since everything is virtual, you can't be making and inventing in person, but we'll make sure you can do it from where you are!

▾ What if I am a complete beginner?

There will be time to plan out your project (design phase) and for execution we plan on providing office hours for you to ask questions and us to guide during the build phase.
